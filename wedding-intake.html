About the Couple
Seven Questions
Question One

When did you first know this was the one?

Not when you decided — when you knew. A specific moment, a look, a sentence. Where were you? What were you doing?

Question Two

What quirk or flaw of theirs do you now love?

Something that might have annoyed anyone else, but became part of why you chose them. The honest ones make the best songs.

Question Three

Was there a hard season — and what got you through?

A time when things weren't easy. You don't have to name what it was — just what it showed you about this person.

Question Four

What is the one promise you most want to make today?

Not "love and cherish forever" — the specific thing you are promising this person. What will you always do?

Question Five

Show me an ordinary moment you would live inside forever.

Not a vacation or a milestone — a Tuesday. The kitchen, a car ride, a couch. What were you two doing?

Question Six

Who were you before them — and who are you now?

Even a sentence. What changed when this person came into your life?

Question Seven

What do you most want them to feel as this song plays?

The feeling the song should leave behind. Seen? Certain? Home? In your own words.

About Your Family
Seven Questions
Question One

What is your earliest memory of her?

A specific scene — her age, where you were, what she was doing. This is where the song will begin.

Question Two

Is there something she always did as a little girl?

A habit, a phrase, a game she invented, the way she laughed — something that was completely and only her.

Question Three

When did you first know she was going to be okay on her own?

The moment you saw who she was becoming. Where were you? What did she do or say?

Question Four

What do you see in the person she chose?

What made you feel at peace about handing her heart to someone else?

Question Five

What have you always wanted her to know?

The thing you've felt but may not have said often enough. This becomes the heart of the song.

Question Six

Complete this sentence: "She taught me…"

What did this daughter teach her father? Something surprising or true about what she gave you.

Question Seven

What is the honest mix of feelings today?

Don't smooth it over. If there's joy and grief together, name both. The best songs hold both at once.
