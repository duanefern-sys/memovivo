What This Is

A Song for Every Moment That Matters

Most couples choose a song from a playlist. A few lucky ones commission something original. MemoVivo makes the original song accessible to every couple — through a simple intake process, professional lyric writing, and AI-assisted music production via Suno.

The result is a fully produced, custom song tailored to that specific couple — their story, their voices, their people. No generic lyrics. No borrowed emotion. Something that, when it plays, makes everyone in the room feel like they're hearing the truth.

And because Suno can generate a full backing track with the AI vocal removed, someone who loves the couple can perform it live — no musical experience required.

Rehearsal Dinner · Best Man or MOH Performs

The Comedy Toast Song
Intake Questions

Completed by the best man, maid of honor, or anyone who knows the couple's story with insider detail. The goal is warmly funny — affectionate roasting that resolves in genuine love. These answers become the raw material for a song that gets the most laughs and the most tears of the entire weekend.

Q1 — The Origin Story

Tell me how these two actually got together — the real version, not the cleaned-up one. What's the detail most people don't know?

This is almost always Verse 1. The more specific and slightly embarrassing, the better the song.

Funny

Q2 — The First Impression

What did [Name] say about [Partner] the very first time? Be honest. What was the exact reaction when they came home or texted you about this person?

The contrast between "first impression" and "now marrying them" is one of the richest comedy structures in a toast song.

Funny

Q3 — The Defining Quirk

What is the one habit, quirk, or recurring behavior of one of them that the other has had to accept as part of the deal?

The most lovable songs name a real imperfection and celebrate it. This is the heart of the song's comedy.

Funny

Q4 — The Worst Trip or Adventure

Describe the most disastrous trip, event, or plan these two attempted together. What went wrong, and how did they handle it?

Shared disasters are the best proof of compatibility. "They survived [that trip] and still chose each other" is a powerful bridge line.

Funny

Q5 — The Running Joke

Is there a phrase, reference, or inside joke between the two of them — or between you and them — that their close circle would immediately recognize?

Even one line that lands with 20 people in the room creates a moment of belonging. Use with care — it must be warm, not exclusive.

Laugh

Q6 — The Moment You Knew It Was Real

At what point did you stop being skeptical and think — okay, this is actually going to happen? What changed your mind?

This is the emotional hinge of the song — the turn from comedy to warmth. It can be funny, tender, or both.

Tender

Q7 — What You'd Say If You Had Thirty Seconds

Set aside the jokes. If you had thirty seconds to tell this couple the most true thing you know about them together — what would you say?

This becomes the song's final chorus or outro. The whole comedy structure exists to earn this moment.

Tender

Q8 — The Performer's Voice

How would you describe your own relationship with this couple — and what's one thing about performing this song that both excites and terrifies you?

This helps us calibrate the voice of the song and write performable lines for someone who may not be a natural singer.

Honest

Reception · Groom–Mother Dance

The Mother's Song
Intake Questions

Completed by the groom's mother — or by the groom himself as a tribute to his mother. One of the most emotionally complex and least-served moments in any wedding. A mother watching her son marry carries pride, release, a lifetime of memory, and a grief she can't quite name. These questions draw that all out.

Q1 — The Earliest Memory

What is the earliest memory you have of him that you can still see clearly — a moment, a look, something he said or did that you have never forgotten?

This is Verse 1. It anchors the song in the beginning of his life so the listener travels the full distance by the end.

Tender

Q2 — The Boy He Was

What did he love most as a little boy — what was the thing he was most himself doing?

Specificity here is everything. "He loved baseball" is generic. "He used to line up all his baseball cards by batting average every Sunday morning" is a lyric.

Tender

Q3 — What He Carried from You

What is something you hoped you gave him — a value, a habit, a way of being in the world — that you actually see in him now?

This is one of the song's most powerful moments: the mother seeing her own investment returned in a grown man.

Honest

Q4 — The Hard Season

Was there a time in his growing up when things were hard — for him or for your family — and you saw something in him that made you believe he'd be okay?

The great mother-son songs don't skip the hard parts. The difficulty is what earns the pride.

Honest

Q5 — When You Knew He'd Found the Right One

At what moment did you know that the person he chose was truly right for him? What did you see?

This belongs in the bridge — the turn from "my son" to "your marriage." It's the handoff, and it needs to be specific.

Tender

Q6 — What He Taught You

Complete this sentence: "I never expected to learn __________ from my own child, but he taught me that."

The reversal — the mother acknowledging what she received from her son — is one of the most powerful lyric moments possible. It shifts the dynamic completely.

Tender

Q7 — What You've Always Wanted Him to Know

If you could say one thing to him today that you have never said perfectly enough — what would it be?

This is the chorus. It must be true enough that hearing it three times feels more true, not less.

Tender

Q8 — The Bittersweet Truth

Describe honestly what today feels like — both the joy and whatever else is in there. Don't just tell me the happy part.

The song must honor both. A mother who says only "I'm so happy" is not telling the whole truth, and the room will feel it. The bittersweet is what makes the song real.

Honest

Q9 — The Blessing

What is the blessing you want to send them into their marriage with — not advice, but a blessing. Something you speak over them.

This becomes the outro. The song should end in blessing, not in sentiment. There is a difference.

Tender

Reception · Performed by Best Man or Maid of Honor

The Toast Song
Intake Questions

This is the toast performed as a song — funnier and more moving than anything a spoken toast alone can deliver. Completed by the best man or maid of honor. Different from the rehearsal dinner comedy song in that it's designed for the larger reception room and aims to travel from laughter to genuine love in under three minutes.

Q1 — How You Know Them

In two sentences: who are you to this couple, and how long have you known the person you're toasting?

This grounds the voice of the song. A best friend of 20 years sings differently than a college roommate of 3 years. The authenticity of the relationship shapes every line.

Honest

Q2 — The Story That Defines Them

What is the single story about this person — before or during this relationship — that tells you everything about who they are?

The best toast songs are built around one story, not a list of qualities. Give us the story and we'll find the song inside it.

Funny

Q3 — What Changed When They Found Each Other

What is different about your friend now that they're with this person? What do you see in them that wasn't there before?

This is the heart of the toast song — the observation that only a close friend can make. "She laughs louder." "He actually shows up on time now." Specifics over adjectives.

Tender

Q4 — The Lovable Absurdity

What is the most absurd, slightly unbelievable, or charmingly ridiculous thing about this couple together that perfectly sums them up?

Every great couple has a thing — a shared weirdness that is completely their own. This detail, sung, gets the biggest laugh and somehow also the biggest feeling.

Laugh

Q5 — The Thing the Partner Doesn't Know

Is there something your friend told you early on about their partner — something they said when they didn't know it was serious yet — that now, looking back, reveals everything?

This is the pre-chorus or bridge material — the revelation moment. "She didn't know it yet, but she was already describing the person she'd marry."

Funny

Q6 — What You Want the Partner to Know

What do you want [Partner's name] to know about the person they're marrying — something only you can tell them?

This is the song's most surprising turn — addressing the partner directly. Done well, it makes the partner cry and the room too.

Tender

Q7 — The Toast

Set aside the microphone for a moment. What do you actually want to say to these two people? Not the funny version — the true version.

This becomes the final chorus or outro. Everything funny in the song exists to earn this moment of truth.

Tender

Q8 — The Performer Note

On a scale of 1–10, how comfortable are you performing in front of people? And: do you want a backing track to sing along to, or are you aiming to have someone else perform while you stand with the couple?

This determines whether we write for a singer-performer or for someone delivering a spoken-song hybrid with musical support. Both are great. We just write differently for each.

Honest

The Production Process

From Memory to Music in Four Steps

MemoVivo handles everything from intake to delivery. The couple or their loved ones answer the questions. We write the lyrics. Suno generates a fully produced track. The vocal is removed and a backing track is delivered — ready for a live performance or played as-is at the event.

01

Intake

The respondent answers the questions for their track — online, at their own pace, in their own words.

02

Lyric Writing

Professional lyrics are written from the intake responses — personalized, singable, emotionally true.

03

Music Production

Suno generates a fully produced song in the requested genre. Multiple versions are created for selection.

04

Backing Track Delivery

The AI vocal is removed. A clean backing track is delivered — ready for a live performer or for the event as-is.
