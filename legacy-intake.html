Seven Questions

Question One

What did people call them?

Their full name, a nickname, what the grandkids called them — whatever felt most like them.

Question Two

What did they love doing most?

Their passion, their hobby, the thing they could do for hours without noticing the time.

Question Three

Is there a place that felt most like them?

A room, a yard, a town, a chair by the window — wherever they seemed most at home in the world.

Question Four

Was there a phrase everyone who loved them would recognize?

A saying, a word they always used, something they repeated so often it became part of who they were.

Question Five

Show me a moment when they were completely happy.

Not a description — a moment. Where were they? Who was there? What were they doing?

Question Six

What small ordinary thing did they always do?

Something so routine nobody talked about it — but everybody knew. The coffee ritual, the way they said goodbye, the sound of them moving through the house.

Question Seven

Because of them, what is different in your life?

Their lasting mark. What did they leave inside you that will still be there ten years from now?
